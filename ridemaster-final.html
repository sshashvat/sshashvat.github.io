"I've tried all three types of riding :- group, with friends, and alone. And each one presents a distinct problem. When riding in a group, you can't signal others effectively, you'd have to call them mid-ride just to get their attention. With friends, no one takes charge, meaning no one is really watching out for the group. And when you ride solo, the most difficult part is that your loved ones at home are left quietly worried because they have no easy way to know that you are okay."
What riders actually use today
Google Maps
Navigation
Turn-by-turn routing. Works flawlessly but only for exactly one person going to exactly one place.
Zero awareness of anyone else on the ride.
WhatsApp
Coordination + relative distance
Share live location in a group chat. "Where are can we stop?" messages. The actual coordination layer most ride groups depend on.
Requires unlocking your phone and reading a message at speed. This is how accidents happen.
Phone calls
Emergency fallback
"Pull over, we lost Ravi." Bluetooth helmets help, but calls still demand full attention at the worst possible moment.
Purely reactive. The problem has already compounded before you can call anyone.
Riders have duct-taped three apps together to do what one should do natively. The gaps only show when something already went wrong, which is exactly the worst moment to have a gap.
How it plays out :- three riding contexts
Group ride
Organised • 4–20 riders
"15 riders leave together. At a fork, the 7th rider follows the wrong car. The tail of the group follows him. Nobody in the front knows for 5 minutes, until someone's phone rings."

No misroute signal. The lead has zero system notification when someone goes off-route. Discovery is accidental.

The tail follows the wrong person. Riders follow whoever is ahead, not the lead. One wrong turn at position 7 fragments everyone behind it.

Recovery is WhatsApp. The group chat explodes mid-ride. Someone has to stop and type. That person who took the wrong turn is now also a hazard.

Friend ride
Casual • 2–5 riders
"Riding with 3 friends on a road trip. You know the route, they don't. One takes a wrong exit. You check your mirror and he's gone. You open WhatsAppand check its location."

No leader, no accountability. Everyone assumes someone else is watching. Nobody is.

WhatsApp live location needs checking. It works only if you switch navigation to whatsapp, find the chat, and read the map. None of that is safe one-handed at 80km/h.

Even basic coordination is a group effort. A fuel stop, food break, or breakdown means opening WhatsApp, pinning a location, waiting for replies or call, all while someone is pulled over on the side of the road.

Solo ride
Independent • unfamiliar routes
"You're out on a long solo ride through an unfamiliar route. Nobody knows exactly where you are. If something goes wrong, there's no one watching."

People back home are worried. There's no passive signal that you're okay. No movement update, no check-in. Just silence until you call or receive a call.

No safety net. A breakdown has no broadcast mechanism. Help depends entirely on being seen by someone who stops.

What's missing across all three

Real-time shared awareness

Knowing where everyone is, and not just yourself. Navigation is solved. Group awareness is not.

Proactive deviation detection

The system should know before the rider does that something has gone wrong and act immediately, not after the fact.

Communication that works in motion

Typing WhatsApp at speed is the accident. Any solution has to work with one hand, in one second, eyes on the road.

These three gaps are :- awareness, proactive detection, in-motion communication. These are what Ridemaster is built to close. Each ride mode addresses the same root problem in a different social context.
Three ride modes
Group ride
Hub and Spoke
One lead rider sets the master route. All riders sync to the same polyline via Firestore real-time listeners. Off-route detection fires per rider, status flag visible to the whole group. Marshal approves or rejects joiners in the waiting room before the ride starts.

All riders render the same blue polyline :-one shared route, no ambiguity

Reroute goes back to the master route, not the destination. Rider rejoins the group to the nearest location

Marshal sees Off Route status flag for any deviated rider in real time

Friend ride
Independent segments
Any friend initiates. The master polyline is copied to every participant's sub-document at join time. When someone deviates, only their segment updates by Directions API called from current position directly to destination. No leader needed, no single point of failure.

Each rider owns their own segment, one person losing track doesn't affect others

Reroute goes to destination directly, friends can take different paths and still arrive together

waiting room but no approval flow to join and ride

Solo ride
Local only • lightweight
Standard navigation with auto-reroute on deviation. No Firestore group sync, significantly lower battery impact. Polyline stored locally. Same map UI as group modes so switching feels seamless.

Emergency contact stored from signup, ready for SOS in v2

No group overhead, faster to start, lighter on battery

Smart alerts

📍 Off-route detection

Distance from polyline exceeds 50m. Red status flag on the group map. Only the deviated rider and the Marshal see the highlight.

🛑 Unexpected stop

Velocity drops below 2m movement threshold for the resting interval. Status flips to "Stopped" in Firestore, visible to the group before anyone has to look back in drawer screen.

↔️ Relative position

Ahead / behind labels computed per rider using progressIndex. Shown as "200m ahead" or "1.2km behind". Zero API cost. Pure Haversine geometry.

Rider status states
Moving Stopped Off Route Pending (Group rides only) Finished

Status computed on-device in the GPS callback, written to Firestore with merge enabled, and read by all riders on the same real-time listener. Pending is Group ride mode only, the Marshal approves before the ride starts.

Problem statement

Group rides have no shared awareness layer, when someone goes off-route, the group has no system-level signal. The only tool is take a stop and check on whatsapp if live location is shared.

Friend rides have no shared route reference and no leader, coordination for every stop, breakdown, or detour falls back to WhatsApp mid-ride.

Solo rides have no passive safety net, people at home have no way to know the rider is okay unless they call.

Constraints
📡

Data required throughout

Active internet needed for the entire ride. No offline mode in v1, real-time sync is core to the product.

🔋

Battery budget

Continuous GPS polling and Firestore writes are expensive. Target less than 15% battery drain per hour over baseline Maps usage.

💰

API cost

Directions API calls are billed per request. Reroutes throttled, only trigger after 50m of movement while off-route.

🛡️

One-handed UX

Every interaction must work with one hand at riding speed. No typing, no multi-step flows during an active ride.

Key product decisions
01
Why Hub-and-Spoke over P2P sync?

Single source of truth in Firestore

P2P sync creates conflict resolution complexity when two riders have different GPS accuracy or brief connectivity drops. Firestore as the central router means one canonical route state that all riders read from. When two devices disagree on the polyline, there is one document to trust.

02
Why 4 signals instead of a chat input?

The riding context makes typing impossible

A rider cannot safely type at speed. The 4-signal set covers every scenario a rider would communicate one-handed. A constrained set also removes ambiguity on the receiving end and the receiver knows exactly what is happening without any interpretation needed.

03
Why is Friend ride architecturally different from Group ride?

Eliminating the single point of failure

In Group mode the lead's device owns the master polyline. If that device loses signal mid-ride, the route is gone for everyone. In Friend mode the base polyline is copied to every participant's sub-document at join time. Each rider owns their own segment, the group keeps moving regardless of who loses connectivity.

Tech stack
Flutter • Dart

App framework

Single codebase for iOS and Android. Dark theme with amber accents. Three main screens :- Home, Past Rides, Profile. SharedPreferences cache check on launch.

Firebase Firestore

Backend + real-time sync

Real-time listeners on the participants sub-collection. SetOptions merge:true prevents field overwrite on every GPS tick. progressIndex only written when non-null.

Google Maps SDK

Maps + routing

Directions API for polylines. Places Autocomplete with 500ms debounce and UUID session tokens which prevents per-keystroke billing.

Geolocator

Adaptive GPS polling

10s when moving (>2m), 60s when stopped. iOS uses AppleSettings with background location. Android uses 10m distance filter.

Haversine formula

Off-route detection

Point-to-segment distance against every polyline segment. Latitude-corrected at 23°N, no flat degree multiplier. Zero API cost.

SharedPreferences

Local persistence

Rider name and emergency contact cached locally. Ride rejoin checks Firestore for existing participant doc, skips info flow if already joined.

North star metric
Ride completion rate
% of rides across all modes that reach "Finished" status for all participants. Covers group, friend, and solo equally. If this goes up, the product is working. If it drops, something fundamental is broken.
Engagement metrics
Engagement
Rides per active user / month

% of users who complete a ride and start another within 30 days. If someone comes back, the product earned it.
Engagement

Guardrail metrics
Guardrail
Waiting room abandonment
Watch: rising
% of riders who join a waiting room but leave before the ride starts. High rate means pre-ride flow has friction or the Marshal never started.
Guardrail
Ghost rider rate
Target: ~0%
% of riders who joined a ride but had zero GPS movement recorded. The location layer silently failed, rider's updates was invisible to everyone
Guardrail
Duplicate reroute rate
Watch: >3 per session
% of rides where Directions API is called 3+ times for the same rider quickly. Signals a logic bug silently burning API cost and battery.
Engineering health
Health
Directions API calls / ride
Watch: cost spike
Tracked in apiUsage.totalCalls. A spike signals reroute logic is triggering redundant calls and burning through quota.
Health
Firestore write failure rate
Target: ~0%
% of GPS broadcasts that land in the catch block in _updateFirestore. A spike means positions silently fail to sync.
Health
Location sync latency (p95)
Target: <10s now
Acceptable at current scale with 10s writes. As group size and concurrent rides grow, this is the first metric that will degrade. A spike here before we change anything means Firestore is struggling.

v2 • Safety layer
Next up

SOS broadcast

One-tap emergency alert. Live location sent to all group riders and the pre-set emergency contact already stored from signup. Trigger: inactivity while stop, no movement for 20mins after a Stopped status.

Next up

Battery awareness

Monitor low-battery riders below 15%. Hold last known location when device goes offline. Show's "Last seen at [location] 4 min ago" on the group map. Android: BatteryManager API. iOS: UIDevice.batteryLevel background access needs validation first.

v3 • Intelligence layer
Planned

Ride history

Simple log of past rides :- route, duration, group size, who rode. Feeds into the existing Past Rides screen already in the app. No new Firestore structure needed, data is already being written.

Planned

Dynamic group ETA

Detect group average speed and recalculate arrival proactively. If the group consistently rides slower than the route assumed, update ETA before it becomes a surprise. Surfaced to Marshal only.

Planned

Custom rider markers

Replace default map pins with personalised markers per rider ie:- name initials, avatar colour, or role badge. Makes the group map readable at a glance when multiple riders are clustered in the same area.

Open questions

iOS battery monitoring in background

iOS restricts UIDevice.batteryLevel when the app is backgrounded. Need to validate whether a background task workaround is feasible before committing to battery awareness in v2.

Firestore scale limit for large groups

Real-time listener cost scales with group size and write frequency. Need load testing beyond 8 simultaneous riders to set a safe cap or evaluate moving large groups to a batched update model.

Optimal GPS polling interval under real conditions

Current polling is fixed at 10s moving / 60s stopped. A speed-adaptive interval could reduce battery drain significantly. Needs field testing across ghat roads, highways, and city traffic before tuning.
