About

8 years in, and the work I find most satisfying is still the same: a messy problem, incomplete data, and figuring out what's actually going on. At MiQ Digital I built data products that went live across four markets and moved $4M+ in revenue. The part I'm proudest of isn't the number, it's that I understood why the products worked, not just that they did.

I've spent the last year deliberately broadening how I think. I wrote a 30-page teardown of India's overdraft market because I wanted to do market sizing and behavioral economics properly, not just talk about them. I built RideMaster solo because I wanted to know what it actually feels like to make product decisions under real constraints. Both taught me about behavioral design, market structure, and what it takes to ship something real on your own.

I'm at a point in my career where I want to go deeper on product ownership. The teardown and the app are evidence of that, not decoration.
